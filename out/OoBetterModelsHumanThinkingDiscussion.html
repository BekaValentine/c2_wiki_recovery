Here are two common ideals about OO that can conflate to misconception level:

  • OO models the RealWorld better
  • OO models human thinking better

Both are nice to keep in mind. But neither are distinct, nor lead to distinctions. A "distinction" is a "definition that continuously defines membership".

If you and I agree on a definition for a distinction about OO designs, then we can always independently and unambiguously determine which OO designs are or are not members of the distinction.

Therefore, I cannot say "my design idea is better than yours because mine models { the real world | human thinking } better."

So these ideals cannot be used to win arguments or defend a given OO design. --PhlIp
