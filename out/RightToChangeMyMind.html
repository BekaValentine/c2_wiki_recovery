Right To Change My Mind

It takes courage to declare I am exercising my RightToChangeMyMind. This ResponsePattern is designed specifically to address the problem associated with SmartPeopleStuckWithBadIdeas.

  • It takes a self-serving and narcissistic attitude conveniently misrepresented as courage. Nevermind the implication that merely because you change your mind or merely because you're stucked with the bad idea you are amongst the "smart people stuck with bad ideas". Who knows maybe you're just among the "boring people stuck with bad idea" variety?
  • Do not "declare it", just do it.
  • Declaring your "right to change your mind", is a nauseating variety of useless text. So what's the big deal if you were wrong and you changed your mind? Good for you, you'll have your place amongst the billions of unsung hero of humanity. Marking your "right to change your mind" as something special is an AntiPattern, it's more about titillating your ego, than serving WikiReaders . By the way, it's not a right because nobody can steal it from you and you don't have to defend it or do anything special to just have it.

The WikiCommunity here strive to achieve and maintain a PositiveDialogueCommunity ideal. This is achieved by having ongoing commitments from smart people with different background and are enthusiastic about sharing viewpoints.

A RightToChangeMyMind exercised appropriately, is a demonstration of the maturity of both the individual/group making such declaration, and the rest of the community who give space for that to happen.

RightToChangeMyMind is a WikiNoisePollution management device.
