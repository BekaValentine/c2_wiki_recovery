Things non-Argentinians know about Argentina

I'm curious, being from Argentina, to know what you guys know about or things that you relate to when you hear the word Argentina. Add items to the list.

  • Borges and a history of cosmopolitanism (not sure what Argentina's like now, after the fiscal policy wildness) and cuisine that includes cow parts that I don't care to eat again.
  • The standard of life is the best one in South America, but it's way behind in economic development/standard of living due to currency/banking/inflation problems.
  • There are a lot of Italian immigrants in Argentina, and the Argentine Spanish has a noticeable Italian resemblance when spoken. Italians and Spaniards were the big wave of immigrants. There are many Germans, Polish, Jewish and Russians as well.
  • The "slang" (Lunfardo) in Argentina is enormous, there are dictionaries with nothing but "Lunfardo" words.
  • Argentina has exported a few dances worldwide; tango is one of them. But the "real" tango is different from the tango we all know. Tango for export is changed a little to appeal to the masses.
  • The musical instrument called the bandoneon has originated in Argentina, I believe. -- ra
    • No - it originated in Germany. But it's heavily used in tango music (BuenosAires's folk music).
  • It is in Argentina - if my memory serves me well - that a group of women come every Thursday downtown with the pictures of loved ones who have disappeared. This is so sad! -- ra
    • Yes. The group of women is called "Madres de Plaza de Mayo" (Mothers of the May Place).
  • There was a huge inflation - and there still is - a few years ago. The country went bankrupt. Ironical since Argent in French means money.. It also means silver... -- ra
