I propose that MarchEighteenth mark the beginning of the annual WikiSpringCleaning.

A mix of deletions and creations improve the wiki

Let's see how long we can keep Wiki below 20,000 without deleting any good content.

Let's see how long we can keep the Wiki vital and relevant by creating good content.

How much good content is already here, and too hard to find behind all the so-so content?

A mechanism called a RoadMap can expose the existing good content by focusing on it and making it more visible.

see: AdaptiveProcess AgileProcess CategoryCategory CategoryRoadMap EjbRoadmap ExtremeProgrammingRoadmap SelfDocumentingCode XpVersionControlRoadmap

Much of the so-so content has already been deleted, and continues to be deleted at a rate of from 5 to 15 pages a day. The emphasis is that vitality and good content come from the exposure of newly created pages. (which will be a mix of many so-so pages and a few outstanding pages). [ update: for the period from April 9, 2002 through March 10, 2003, over 3400 pages were added to the Repository, that averages 10 pages per day) At this years end (February 2004, we can expect the total to be somewhere between 25500-27500.)]

Perhaps effort toward creating new RoadMaps would not be a waste.
