Retrospective Buffer

The name of a mythical data structure that buffers bits from the future.

You have to buffer information from the future for later processing to avoid violating the "Temporal Prime Directive" of Star Trek. Otherwise you might suffer from time loops and paradoxes. And we all know that a time loop can be just as bad as an infinite loop. ;->

Like a calendar, or todo list? Ooops, that'd be bits about the future.
