Learning Style

People are all different. But these differences come from the same source. This source is sometimes called Learning Styles. These styles have been described, amongst others, by Kolb.

When learning, people have to make a choice between being Active (like trying something) or Reflective (let's pause and think first). People also have to make a choice between Concrete (what is it, how does it feel?) and Abstract (why is this, what rules apply?). So the four LearningStyles are:

  • ActiveConcrete
  • ReflectiveConcrete
  • ReflectiveAbstract
  • ActiveAbstract

For an orthogonal concept, see also

  • BreadthFirstLearning
  • AstarSearchLearning, i.e. AstarSearch applied to what you learn (discussed briefly on BreadthFirstLearning)
  • DepthFirstLearning

And perhaps related but I'm not sure (yet?) exactly how,

  • ReciprocalityTheory
  • MappersVsPackers -- on interactions between two different types of learner
