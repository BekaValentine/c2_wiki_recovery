Why Nobody Deletes Wiki

  • Most people are not really evil-minded at all!
  • Nobody's crazy enough yet to do so.
  • Wiki isn't easy to find unless you are told about it or are searching for it.
  • Why vandalize because you're bored and/or ignored when you can write and be neither?
  • Deletion through boredom is boring. The infinite boredom loop causes InstantDeath.
  • What's the point when you know that someone else will just undo it immediately after you are gone?
  • Most people don't even notice the EditText link.
  • People have better things to do.
  • If there's no interest in un-deleting a page then it SHOULD be deleted because nobody cared about it.
  • People who delete stuff maliciously are lazy.
  • Vandalism that is permitted ceases to be vandalism. With no rules to break, no power to challenge, what's the point?
  • Why trade something for nothing?
  • Until Wikipedia, only a small fraction of humanity knew about Wikis.
  • Because it is easier to read than to write?
  • It's free information, right there waiting for you when you most need it. Why get rid of it?
  • Deletions can be easily reverted, so vandalism is pointless.

This misses the fundamental problem of:

  • It's not that I would WANT to delete pages, but how do I prevent 2 users from walking all over each other?
  • It's almost always easier to destroy than it is to create. This is the reality of the world! We've probably all gone through phases in our lives when we like to destroy. Haven't you ever broken a window, or something, just for the fun of it? (Probably not when it was your window, or if you'd have to fix it.) But, as we get older it seems like a silly thing to do, and it becomes much more fun to create.
    • See EditConflictResolution
  • allow for UNDO, and go back to previous versions?
    • Regulars know how to do this
  • allow for merge?
    • That's left to the person making the more recent change
  • It's not about malice, it's about accidents.

See VersioningWouldHelpNewbies and WikiFailures for discussion of these features.
