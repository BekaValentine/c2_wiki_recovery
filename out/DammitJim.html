When I started my job in 1989 the programmers were the PC Techs, System Administrators and the Network People (I was even known to run some fanfold paper through the burster). As time has gone by we have increased in size and diversified (we went from 5 people to 20) but people still come to me with issues because they know I can fix them even if it is not my job. It is hard to resist the temptation to play guru and fix every little problem that comes my way. I try hard to defer to the people who are responsible because it is not fair for me to work their turf because I get paid to program and they get paid to do their job(even if they themselves don't mind). -- TobyFarley
