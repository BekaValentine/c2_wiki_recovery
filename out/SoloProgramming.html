Solo Programming

SoloProgramming requires just one person writing code sat all alone in front of their workstation. You know - the usual thing.

Not a very exciting or new idea, but one I thought that ought to have a name, in the same way that if you're not a Twin, or a Triplet, etc.., you're a Singleton.

--StephenHutchinson
