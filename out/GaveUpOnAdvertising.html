Gave Up On Advertising

Some people on this wiki GaveUpOnTelevision. Has anyone done the same with advertising?

Avoiding advertising is harder than avoiding television. You have to:

  • Avoid commercial television (or use TiVo)
  • Avoid commercial radio
  • Avoid ad-supported magazines and newspapers
  • Use an ad-filtering Web proxy (JunkBuster, Privoxy, etc.)
  • Arrive late to movie theaters late to miss pre-movie commercials
  • Avoid adware
  • Get on your state/province/canton's no-call list

Has anyone else tried this? What are your experiences?

I probably fit the bill, but this was not a goal so much as a side-effect. I have very little exposure to adds for the following reasons: I

  • don't watch TV
  • don't listen to commercial radio
  • don't subscribe to any ad-supported media
  • don't go to many movies, and those I do tend to be at a local independent theatre -- no ads.
  • don't drive -- no billboards
  • don't run any windows machines -- adware doesn't get to me
  • run a proxy server -- ad filtering on the web
  • filter my email and avoid propagating real address -- (nearly) no spam

I didn't choose to do any of things things to avoid ads, except the proxy server. The lack of ads in my life is merely an added bonus! I do, however, find that I am sufficiently removed from the ad culture (for going on 10 years now) that when I am exposed to advertising it often seems surreal.
