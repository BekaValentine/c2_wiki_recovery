Pi Equals Three

For large values of three, and small values of pi.

If you don't need the accuracy (was that temperature 12 000 000 C or 12 000 000 K?), and can get away with an approximation to an OrderOfMagnitude, pi = 3 and pi^2 = 10.

Or rather pi^2 = g; cancels out nicely in physics courses. But for such approximations, g also equals 10. :-)

See also ValueOfPi

"The value of 'pi', recited to the length of one decimal digit...

"3".
