The idea of sound via the web is a concept that is here to stay, and whether or not Associations and Companies and Special Interests like it or not, good ideas will not be squelched. Innovation and progress will continue and if the RIAA and others think the internet is where they will pick up some more big bucks, they need to think again. If I were in their shoes, I would think of the internet as a pool of people to which I can advertise and spread samples of my product, which they will buy. There is a great deal of difference between listening to Cds and owning Cds. The second is where the money is. You do not coerce ownership, you induce it. Secondly, the covenience of the new format (Mp3) in that it makes more sound per square inch of Cd or Chips, and should be used as an instrument of widening audiences and making sound ownership less expensive and more profitable. The market possibilites are huge. The business models that recognize these and other obvious strengths and possibilities will be the survivors.
