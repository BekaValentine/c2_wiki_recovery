Staple Pattern

Illustrated by excerpt: StaplePattern -- ThinkingOutLoud DonaldNoyes 20070130

I delve through the code base as I prepare to maintain. I notice the use of functions and macros, classes and defines peppered throughout the code. They may be a class, in a function, or a namespace, and it is not descriptive: it is a 'helper'. Helper is a code smell. You don't understand the problem, only that the solution in its current form requires help. A suggestion: choose a more helpful name.

The coup de grace comes when those enamored by the patterns movement find themselves smitten with helpers -- snippets of code who do nothing on their own and barely something when composed -- and suggest they are grand pieces of an architectural puzzle: proxy-helper-singleton-visitor; pub-sub-impl-pimple-lightweight-provider. Understand the problem better and describe better the solution. Choose better names; helpers are not helpful!
