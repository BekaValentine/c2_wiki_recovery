Increasing Your Ward Rating

At XpUniverse, Kent came up with an off-the-wall idea, the Ward Rating or Degree of Wardness (or WardNumber on this Wiki). He designated Ward as 0, and rated people who had paired with Ward as 1, people who had paired with people who had paired with Ward as 2, and so on. You wrote your Ward Rating on your conference badge. JosephPelrine noticed an interesting phenomenon and, together with AlanFrancis (and much beer) came up with an (just slightly) crazy idea.

By the end of the conference, there were quite a few folks walking around with their chests puffed out proudly, showing that their Ward Rating of 3 or 4 or more had gone up a notch or two, since they had the chance to pair with others who had more experience. Since JosephPelrine considers XP to be an orally transmitted tradition, he found this to be a GoodThing.

A few other thoughts occurred to him:

  • An XP Fest (analog to OOPSLA's design and code fest, but without the excess baggage) took place, and the participants built real software for a real, non-profit organization
  • Both tennis players and chess players go to tournaments to play against others in order to increase their ranking.

So, putting this all together, JosephPelrine and AlanFrancis had the following idea: why not organize some kind of XP Fest at conferences , where folks can come along and pair with others on non-profit projects, while at the same time, in a sense of collaboration rather than competition, they have the chance to raise their Ward Rating. It would also help us as a community, since we get more people introduced to the "real" XP. We're going to need that in a few years, when XP finally takes off, and everyone will want to ride the bandwagon ;-)

- JosephPelrine
