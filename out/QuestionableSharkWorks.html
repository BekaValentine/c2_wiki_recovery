It's a bit of human nature I've seen before: when a piece of forum software rejected posts that had a high mispelling-to-overall-length ratio, people started padding forum posts with unneeded sentences "to get around the spellchecker". Also, it discourages dyslexics from contributing (since needed corrections are opposed).

While that's possible, I would hope folks would not pad their edits in an attempt to circumvent the SharkBot.

  • First, it won't work.
  • Second, SharkBot maintains a whitelist of IP addresses associated with legitimate edits. I've already been adding IP addresses to it as folks contribute content or do obviously non-GrammarVandal gnoming. You can email me (see above) to have your IP address(es) manually added.

Corrections of dyslexic spelling are less likely to trigger the SharkBot than GrammarVandal's specific modus operandi, anyway.   • Fifth, over time SharkBot's ability to discriminate GrammarVandal from legitimate edits will improve.
