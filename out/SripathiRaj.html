I am Sripathi Guruprasannaraj, a Masters student in the State University of New York at Buffalo. I recently came to know about Wiki when I was at the Yet Another Perl Conference YAPC. For a person who likes to write a lot and help out other people while gaining knowledge myself, the wiki has kept me engaged already.

My area of specialization is InformationRetrieval and NaturalLanguageProcessing. My other love is ProgrammingLanguages. I started out learning Visual Basic but some Godsent guy told me to learn C. So I learnt C when I was in high school and moved on to C++ though I kinda preferred C. When I was in college, we had Fortran, Pascal, COBOL. I taught myself these languages an year before they were to be taught and I was always ahead of the pack which made me very disinterested in class. And then Java came along though I dint take it up right away. I had an accident on my bike and was bed-ridden for like two weeks. I got really bored and when I came back from the hospital, I was looking for something to read which was when I noticed my Java Complete Reference book. I realised that I hadnt read it after I bought it and decided that this was the time to start studying Java. And then I was hooked on to it for like 2 years. Meanwhile my interests wavered from Assembly Language to ASP.

Perl is a very powerful language that I feel anyone interested in programming should learn. I have done some really good projects with it and I feel that in whatever I have done, I have used probably 0.5% of Perl's power. I currently teach Java to Non-Majors at the University at Buffalo and its been a pretty good experience so far. I have taken up this assignment for the summer and am looking for a job as Perl/Java Developer. I am into J2EE now and am planning to take the Sun Certification exams. In an aim to further my database skills, I am also planning to write the Oracle certification exam. If you have any tips for me on these exams, leave them here or mail them to me at sg47@cse.buffalo.edu.

It was nice to get started here and I'll be coming back tomorrow to post about a big project I am planning to take up. I'll need you guys' help on that.
