The Creative Process

MrAristotle

  • Metaphysics

Joseph Wallas (1926)

  • Preparation
  • Incubation
  • Illumination
  • Verification

Joseph Rossman (1931)

  • Observation of a need or difficulty
  • Analysis of a need
  • A survey of all available information
  • A formulation of all objective solutions
  • A critical analysis of these solutions
  • The birth of the new idea - the invention
  • Experimentation to test out the most promising solution

Osborn (1953)

  • Orientation: pointing up the problem
  • Preparation: gathering pertinent data
  • Analysis: breaking down the relevant material
  • Ideation: piling up alternative by way of ideas
  • Incubation: "letting up" - to invite illumination
  • Synthesis: putting the pieces together
  • Evaluation: judging the resulting ideas

Taylor (1959)

Wallas's four stages + five levels:

  • Expressive creativity
  • Productive creativity
  • Inventive creativity
  • Innovative creativity
  • Emergent creativity

Morris Stein (1967, 1974)

Three stages:

  • Hypothesis formulation
  • Hypothesis testing
  • Communication of results

Guilford (1950, 1957, 1959)

  • Divergent Thinking

Koestler (1964)

  • Bisociation
