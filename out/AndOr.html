English 'or' sometimes means 'inclusive-or' and sometimes means 'exclusive-or'. When the meaning is not clear from context, speakers will use "any of" or "one or more of" or '"at least one of" to mean 'inclusive-or' and "either X or Y, but not both" to mean 'exclusive-or'. Most people are not logicians, and the ambiguity doesn't matter much in everyday speech.
