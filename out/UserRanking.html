User Ranking

User Ranking fixes what is commonly seen on sites that have high community participation and decentralized editorship: ThreadMess. Without knowing who is credible, one hardly knows whom to listen to in the masses. AdvoGato helped pave the way for this by conceiving a reliable TrustMetric (similar to PageRank).

UserRanking is a companion to PerItemVoting and forms the orthogonality necessary to make a PeerToPeer platform scale.

How does that protect unpopular people from being ranked away into oblivion? If Galileo came into a forum with 80% Catholics, for example, how would it protect him? (True, Bozo the Clown may also keep visiting, and there may be a good reason to keep him out.)

  • There is an easy solution to this. I call it the ProbabilisticChooser pattern. It's based on GeneticAlgorithms and evolutionary theory: you don't just choose the best each time, you choose from a weighted probability of every option.
  • That's an approach sometimes used on Web sites that let users rank pictures, messages, user uploads and the like, to make sure unpopular content stays unpopular but unfamiliar content gets some love. The weighting is often skewed toward most-recently-added content, to give it a chance to become popular. Not sure how it's based on GeneticAlgorithms and evolutionary theory, though. Where are the chromosomes? Where is the fitness function?
  • I used the word "based" [on GAs], but what I really meant is that it comes out of my own work in GAs. It's part of the selection function that chooses what genes get propagated between generations.
