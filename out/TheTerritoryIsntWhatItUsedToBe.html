The Territory Isnt What It Used To Be

In the spirit of the TheMapHasReplacedTheTerritory, Let me offer: TheTerritoryIsntWhatItUsedToBe.

Meaning -- At a given time, a representation, map, chart, description, definition, etc. may be a good illustration of of an entity or process, but given the passing of time and the change that it introduces, What was once the territory, is no longer what it was. (ala PersonalComputer, Laptop, Linux, Unix, Windows, WikiWiki, the old neighborhood, that brand new car without a scratch or dent, your opinions and beliefs, The Internet, your waistline, the picture on your driver's license) It just isn't the same! --DonaldNoyes

"First, a bit of etymology. The word, 'metaphor' is a Greek composition, consisting of 'meta' (over or beyond) + 'phor' (to carry). So, literally, to carry beyond. How does that map to our current usage of the term? I'm not sure, but I think it means we take the literal, directly observable, and treat it as a symbol for something else, thus carrying it beyond its literal meaning."
