Wiki Front Door

FrontPage, WelcomeVisitors and WikiWikiWeb overlap a lot. This could be confusing for new visitors, and OnceAndOnlyOnce is probably in order to provide visitors with a single, straightforward and welcoming WikiFrontDoor, that leads cleanly to starting points and new user help.

How would people go about refactoring the existing three pages in order to minimize or eliminate redundancy and accentuate clarity? Or do you disagree with the idea in the first place? I disagree entirely. The three pages are mostly overlap, and disjointed overlap at that. ThinkingOutLoud: Perhaps the three pages should be modified to clarify purpose and reinforce each other by confining the content in the following manner:

For example:

  • FrontPage - About Pages - how the WikiWikiWeb is organized using pages and spell out how pages are created, how they are changed, and how pages can be related to one another. Included could be pointers to Roadmaps and to how the Category system works.
  • WelcomeVisitors - About People - could be about how the visitor can become familiar with the interaction mechanisms, the creation of a HomePage, what passes for acceptable and unacceptable behaviour and introduction to how Stewards can help in correcting abuses and inappropriate behaviours. Here also could be introduction to ways a visitor can get to know something of the people and projects which have become part of the history of WikiWikiWeb.
  • WikiWikiWeb - About Software - that makes this Wiki work, and the ongoing Emphasis on People, Projects, Patterns, Extreme and Agile Methodology, and Programming Languages and Methods.

-- DonaldNoyes
