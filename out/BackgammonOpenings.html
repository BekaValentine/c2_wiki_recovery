Backgammon Openings

Your style of play, running or blocking, will be dictated by board developments that cannot be anticipated on the first roll. Therefore: Play this roll toward points which have strength in either game, specifically your own 5 point or its neighbors. In order, try to ...

  • Make your 5, 7, or 4 point, or
  • Split from your 6 to your 5 point, play the other die off of your opponent's 12, or
  • Leap to your 5 point from your opponent's 12, or
  • Open up off of your opponent's 12

With this aggressive play you get a valuable point, start the most valuable point, or at least get builders for these points. With the 5 point, or its neighbor, you have a doorway home for your running game and a good start on a prime for your back game.

Backgammon authors recommend you memorize the best moves for all 15 possible initial rolls. This pattern generates their recommendations.

  • Backgammon for Blood, Bruce Becker isbn 0380003848 -- I wouldn't play a game for anything other than fun; drawing blood just spoils things ;-)
  • Paradoxes and Probabilities, Barclay Cooke isbn 0394501268

The moves generated by the pattern above are not necessarily the best ones. Expert opinion (prompted by the development of computer programs that play backgammon at about the same level as the best humans, but not always in the same ways) has shifted away from playing 6/5 with opening 1s and towards 24-23. And almost all experts would play 8/3 6/3 with an opening 53. Oh, and the 65 it proposes (13/7 13/8) is terrible.

A set of opening moves that would meet with current expert approval is generated by the following rules. As with Ward's, you should follow the first piece of advice you can.

  • Make 3, 4, 5 or 7. [31 42 53 61]
  • Run with 24/13. [65]
  • Split off 24 with a 1 or 6; play the other move from 13. [21 41 51 62 63 64]
  • Build by playing both rolls off 13 [32 43 52 54]

With 52, it is probably better to play 24/22 13/8, but I couldn't find a way to recommend that without needing more rules. :-)
