A comic-book introduction to LispLanguage, using AllegroCommonLisp for the example code.

The author entreated CommonLisp users and implementors to forget the term "macro" and replace it with "spel," because of macro's semantic load.

"SPEL is certainly closer to the truth about Lisp macros. A SPEL enlarges your active vocabulary, but it drains your Mana during debugging. Very appropriate." -- Markus Fix
