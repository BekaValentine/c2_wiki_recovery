Borges Classification Of Animals

EditHint: note TheOrderOfThings

This passage quotes "a certain chinese encyclopedia", the (completely fictional) "Celestial Emporium of Benevolent Knowledge", in which it is written that animals are divided into:

  1. those that belong to the Emperor,
  2. embalmed ones,
  3. those that are trained,
  4. suckling pigs,
  5. mermaids,
  6. fabulous ones,
  7. stray dogs,
  8. those included in the present classification,
  9. those that tremble as if they were mad,
    1. innumerable ones,
    2. those drawn with a very fine camelhair brush,
    3. others,
    4. those that have just broken a flower vase,
    5. those that from a long way off look like flies.
