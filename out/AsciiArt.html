Hey I See It!!!

To see the heart, have the screen at a comfortable reading distance, then focus on an imagined image well behind the screen. Getting your eyes to focus on something you can't see until you've done it takes a little practice, but is not too difficult. You must have reasonable vision with both eyes. One the heart is visible, it will appear to have the "background text" a few inches behind it. It would more impressive (and more difficult) to use a proper AsciiArt picture instead of just a rough heart-shape.

This way works easier for me. If you look closely, you'll notice a vertical banded pattern in the "block". If you manage to "de-focus" your sight (seeing double) and overlap two contiguous bands, you'll see the stereogram.
