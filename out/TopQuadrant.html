Top Quadrant

TopQuadrant is where you can now find RalphHodgson. We are a US-based consulting company working in the "Solution Design" space addressing the translation gap between business strategy and IT solutions. With the many solution choices in the industry, development is no longer pure "green-field" but more about selecting and integrating components, innovating where clear business value will derive. We are using solution envisioning methods along with a database of solution "Capability Cases" with technologies, vendors and products situated expressed in a business problem context. The method, v2rM, "Vision to Reality" Method, is scenario-based. Scenarios are annotated with forces and desired results. These are mapped to solution capabilities expressed in a pattern language.

To restate In anotherForm: With the many solution choices (available) in the industry development is no longer pure "GreenField" but more about

  • SelectingAndIntegratingComponents
  • Innovating where ClearBusinessValue will be realized
  • Using SolutionEnvisioningMethods
  • With
    • A DatabaseOfSolutionCapabilityCases
    • TechnologiesVendorsAndProducts situated/expressed in a BusinessProblemContext.

The method, v2rM, VisionToRealityMethod, is ScenarioBased.

  • Scenarios are AnnotatedWithForcesAndDesiredResults.
  • These are mapped to SolutionCapabilities
  • Expressed in a PatternLanguage.

A UseCase is a "Case of Use", a CapabilityCase is "the Business Case for a Capability"

CapabilityCases act as memes and provide a vocabulary for TradingSolutionConcepts in the early stages of envisioning a solution.

Examples of CapabilityCase are:

  • InstantMessager -- well-known and oft-used example of a solution concept meme, there was a time when this was not the case
  • Recommender -- becoming more prevalent
  • ConceptBasedSearch -- becoming known
  • ContextAwareRetriever -- making information available to a user based on context and intent
  • PersonalizedNewsletter -- myNews

A BusinessCase is a ValueProposition expressed as

  • Benefits (aka results, aka outcomes) to StakeHolders,
  • Forces (drivers) that are addressed
  • Challenges (internal effects of forces) are overcome
    • using solution stories as analogies (DesignByAnalogy)catalysts
