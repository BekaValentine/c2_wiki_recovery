Xp Is Result Of Applying Business Process Reeng To Sw Eng Process

This is a thought I just had. It may or may not have any merit. Also, it is probably not well communicated. I would appreciate help developing this thought.

ExtremeProgramming is the result of applying BusinessProcessReengineering (as defined by HammerAndChampy as described in Reengineering the Corporation : A Manifesto for Business Revolution, ISBN:088730687X) to the SoftwareEngineeringProcess. My rationale outlined:

  1. BPR means drastic and fundamental changes to existing processes in order to archieve order of magnitude performance improvements by contemporary measures. Xp seems to be a drastic and fundamental change. I would like to see if it gives a order of magnitude performance improvement.
  1. BPR's focus is on serving the customer. Xp seems to do this quite well through UserStories.
  1. BPR says that a company needs to move away from tasks and toward processes. This seems to meets Xp's believe that developers should work on making a UserStory work, not just making a particular module work for a UserStory.
  1. BPR says that a large portion of the work can be done by one or two people (PairProgramming) and that only a small portion of the work requires expert help (ala TheCoach or ChiefArchitect).
  1. BPR says that you should supply all the information needed so that one or two people can complete an entire process. This seems to work in with CrcSessions and the entire engineering team being in that session.
  1. BPR says change is the norm, not the exception. This seems to be one of the core principles driving Xp.
  1. BPR says that process should be minimal. If there is an exception then a new minimal process should be developed to handle the exception. This seems to be the case with Xp (MinimalMethodology, understanding how the process is performing and feeding that back into the process, JustRules, . . .).

Maybe I have taken the analogy too far, but it seems to match OneToOne. Input?
