World Net

The World Net, the dream of early visionaries on the ARPANET, is not the internet. The World Net isn't a network of machines but of human beings, it is a human-net linking every human being on Earth to every other, without intermediaries. The World Net is to the internet what democracy is to elections; they have nothing to do with each other.

I don't think translation software has to do as well as humans to be reliably useful. Years ago, post 2000 probably, there was an article in Wired about Top 10 Worst Things To Come or something. One of the items on the list was that voice recognition's problem with accents had been "solved" by everyone learning a standardized monotone version of spoken English. The same could be applied to translation software if you provide users with enough feedback to modify their own behaviour. If translation software automatically and in real time provided double translations of what you're writing, that would constitute such feedback.
