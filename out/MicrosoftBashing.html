The comment you responded to was from someone not keeping up with the times. Originally, Bourne-shell family shells were best for scripting and worst for interactive, and vice versa with csh-family shells -- so the cogniscenti interacted with a csh-family shell and wrote scripts in Bourne-shell family shells. Eventually, however, bash added all of the csh/tcsh/zsh/ABCXYZsh features, so these days it's great for both scripts and for interactive use (although there are a few C-shell-isms, syntax-wise, that I was sad to lose, but that's a minor thing). The generalizations of GNU readline, and its separation as a library and thus reuse in many other programs, is further icing on the cake. But now this is really off-topic. -- DougMerritt
