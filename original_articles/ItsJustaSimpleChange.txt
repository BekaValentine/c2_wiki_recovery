One of the reasons given for not PairProgramming.  Here are some arguments against this.

	* Everything we do should be simple.
	* Most things that appear to be simple turn out to be icebergs.
	* How do you distinguish between "simple" and "not simple"?

In response to this statement from my team, I offered them a compromise.  They would be permitted to fly solo on what they considered to be simple changes as long as they agreed to support their code 24/7.  In other words, throw CollectiveCodeOwnership out the window.  This didn't seem to be very appealing for some reason.  -  KenReigle
